Julian day number

In 1582, between March 11 and March 21, precisely zero people were born in the world! These days were skipped when the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregorian_calendar, Gregorian calendar] replaced the [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_calendar, Julian calendar].
The [http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julian_day, Julian day number] (JDN) is still used by astronomers; it is the number of days since noon on January 1, 4713 BC. Below we [http://www.cambridge.org/features/astronomy/, compute the JDN] and apply it to find the day of the week.
